               3.6.2  Uniflow Scavenge System

               There are also two types of uniflow scavenging system. These are:

               (a)  Uniflow Valve Scavenging
               The uniflow valve scavenging system shown in Fig. 3-9 (c) is used in high-power output low-speed
               marine diesel engine and in light high-speed engines. The scavenge air enters the cylinders through
               ports located around the entire periphery of the cylinders and the burnt gases are expelled via the
               valve(s) located in the cylinder head or cover. The scavenge air passes through the cylinders in a
               straight flow. To ensure preliminary exhausting of the gases, the exhaust valve(s) open before the
               scavenge ports. When the cylinder is being charged with additional air, these valves are closed. This
               type of scavenge system is used in the Sulzer RTA series and the Burmister and Wain-MAN diesel
               engines.

               (b) Uniflow Port Scavenging
               This type shown in Fig.3-9 (d) is used in opposed piston or double acting two-stroke cycle engines.
               The scavenge air enters through the lower ports and the burnt gases are exhausted through the upper
               ports or vice versa. The scavenge ports are located tangentially to the cylinder circumference and
               somewhat inclined to its axis. This creates spiral motion of the air charge and improves scavenging
               of the cylinders.
