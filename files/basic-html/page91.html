7.2.3  Fuel Injection Sequence
               Fuel  at  high  pressure  is  fed  from  the  injection  pump  through  the  drilling  in  the  holder,  via  the
               annular groove and drillings in the nozzle body to the reservoir as shown in Fig. 7.2. The nozzle
               valve  is  raised  from  its  seat  by  the  fuel  pressure  acting  on  the  differential  area  formed  by  the
               reduced diameter, overcoming the spring tension, and the fuel is forced through the holes as a fine
               spray  into  the  combustion  chamber.  When  the  pump  cuts  off  the  fuel  supply,  the  pressure  falls
               rapidly  and  the  spring  returns  the  nozzle  valve  to  its  seat.  A  slight  leakage  of  fuel  takes  place
               between the nozzle valve body and the holder around the internal bore and lubricates the valve.
               Excess fuel is led away via the leak off connection and piping.

               The type of nozzles to be used is determined by the engine manufacturers and it depends on the
               particular requirement of the engine performance and the combustion chamber. Since nozzle design
               varies considerably with respect to its diameter, length, spray angle valve lift, etc. It is essential that
               only the specified nozzles are used in service.
